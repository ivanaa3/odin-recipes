Description

When Courtney Page Ferrell set out to find her grandmother's long-lost apple pie recipe, she got much more than she bargained for. The Virginia-based creative consultant and sought-after speaker put up 'Lost Pie' signs around her neighborhood (and other cities, when she was traveling) and received a slew of recipes and stories from other apple pie lovers. This delicious double-crust old-fashioned apple pie is the closest she came to her grandmother's beloved recipe.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • All-purpose flour: The base of the crust. Be sure to scoop flour into the measuring up with a spoon and level when measuring for consistent results.
  • Unsalted butter or cold vegetable shortening: Use whichever you prefer or a combination of both (see below).
  • Kosher salt: Enhances the flavor of the crust. Leave out if using salted butter.

For the filling:

  • Tart apples: We like Granny Smith or McIntosh, but feel free to use whichever type(s) of apples you prefer.
  • Granulated sugar: You can also use light or dark brown sugar for subtle notes of caramel.
  • All-purpose flour: Helps thicken the filling. Can be swapped with cornstarch or other preferred thickeners.
  • Ground cinnamon: Spices up the filling for a warming touch.
  • Kosher salt: Enhances the flavor of the apples.
  • Unsalted butter: Adds richness to the filling.

Steps

  1. Mix together flour, butter, and salt in a large bowl with your fingertips until butter is well incorporated. Gradually sprinkle 2 to 4 tablespoons very cold water into mixture, kneading as you add water, until dough just comes together. Knead dough to a smooth ball on a lightly floured surface. Divide and shape into two disks. Wrap and chill 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Let 1 disk stand at room temperature about 5 minutes. Roll into a 12-inch circle and fit into a 9-inch pie plate. Chill.
  3. Preheat oven to 425°F with oven rack in lowest position. Stir together apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, and salt. Spoon into piecrust; sprinkle with butter.
  4. Roll remaining disk to a 12-inch circle. Cut into 12 strips and arrange in lattice pattern over apples. Trim and crimp edges.
  5. Place pie onto a rimmed baking sheet. Bake 15 minutes. Reduce temperature to 350°F and bake 45 minutes. Cover loosely with aluminum foil if needed, and bake another 30 minutes. Cool on a wire rack at least 1 hour before serving.
